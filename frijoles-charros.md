# Frijoles Charros
### Mexican Pinto Beans With Bacon and Chiles
_based on_ https://www.seriouseats.com/frijoles-charros-mexican-pinto-beans-bacon-recipe

### Ingredients

- 2 lbs. dried pinto beans
- Kosher salt
- 12c. homemade or store-bought low-sodium chicken stock
- 4 bay leaves
- 4 sprigs epazote (optional)
- 24 oz. diced bacon
- 2 medium white or yellow onions, diced
- 4 serrano chiles or 2 jalapeños, minced
- 6 medium cloves garlic, minced
- 4 (14-oz.) cans diced fire-roasted tomatoes
- 2 large handfuls chopped fresh cilantro leaves and fine stems

## Method

1. Place the beans in a large bowl and fill with enough cold water to cover by at least 4". Add 4 Tbsp. kosher salt and stir to dissolve. Let soak 8 to 12 hours. Drain and rinse.
2. In a large Dutch oven, add the beans, stock, bay leaves, 4 tsp. kosher salt, and epazote (if using). Bring to a boil over high heat, reduce to a bare simmer, cover, and cook until the beans are just tender, about 45 minutes.
3. Meanwhile, heat the bacon in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook, stirring constantly, until the fat is rendered and the bacon is just starting to brown around the edges, about 5 minutes. Add the onion and chiles and cook, stirring, until softened and just starting to brown, about 4 minutes. Add the garlic and cook, stirring, until fragrant, about 30 seconds. Add the fire-roasted tomatoes and cook, stirring and scraping up browned bits from the bottom of the pan, until the liquid is thick and the mixture begins to sizzle, about 3 minutes.
4. Remove the lid from the Dutch oven, add the bacon-tomato mixture, and continue cooking, stirring occasionally, until the beans are completely creamy and the liquid has thickened into a rich broth, about 20 minutes. Season to taste with salt, discard the bay leaves, stir in cilantro, and serve.
