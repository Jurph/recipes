# Frijoles Charros
### Mexican Pinto Beans With Bacon and Chiles
_based on_ https://www.seriouseats.com/frijoles-charros-mexican-pinto-beans-bacon-recipe

### Ingredients

- 2 lbs. dried pinto beans
- Kosher salt
- 12c. homemade or store-bought low-sodium chicken stock
- 4 bay leaves
- 4 sprigs epazote (optional)
- 24 oz. diced bacon or pork belly 
- 2 medium white or yellow onions, diced
- 4 serrano chiles or 2 jalapeños, minced
- 8+ medium cloves garlic, minced
- 4 (14-oz.) cans diced fire-roasted tomatoes
- 2 large handfuls chopped fresh cilantro leaves and fine stems

## Method

1. Place the beans in a large bowl and fill with enough cold water to cover by at least 4". Add 1/4c. kosher salt and stir to dissolve. Let soak 8 to 12 hours. Drain and rinse.
2. In a large Dutch oven, add the beans, stock, bay leaves, 4 tsp. kosher salt, and epazote (if using). Bring to a boil over high heat, reduce to a bare simmer, cover, and cook until the beans are just tender, about 45 minutes.
3. Meanwhile, heat the bacon in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook, stirring constantly, until the fat is rendered and the bacon is just starting to brown around the edges, about 5 minutes. Transfer to a larger pot and add the onion and chiles. Cook, stirring, until softened and just starting to brown, about 4 minutes. Add the garlic and cook, stirring, until fragrant, about 30 seconds. Add the fire-roasted tomatoes and cook, stirring and scraping up browned bits from the bottom of the pan, until the liquid is thick and the mixture begins to sizzle, about 3 minutes.
4. Remove the lid from the Dutch oven, pluck out the bay leaves and epazote stems, then add the bacon-tomato mixture and stir to combine. Place under a broiler for 20-60 minutes, or until a scorched top layer forms, adding smokiness. Remove from under the broiler, add the cilantro, and stir to combine.
5. Reduce oven heat to 205F, cover, and let simmer until ready to serve.
