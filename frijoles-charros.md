# Frijoles Charros
### Mexican Pinto Beans With Bacon and Chiles
_based on_ https://www.seriouseats.com/frijoles-charros-mexican-pinto-beans-bacon-recipe

### Ingredients

- 2 lbs. dried pinto beans 
- Kosher salt 
- 2 quarts homemade or store-bought stock (chicken, pork, whatever) 
- 4 bay leaves
- 4 sprigs epazote (optional)
- 24 oz. diced bacon or pork belly 
- 2 medium white or yellow onions, diced
- 4 serrano chiles or 2 jalapeños, minced 
- 8+ medium cloves garlic, minced or microplaned 
- 4 (14-oz.) cans diced fire-roasted tomatoes
- 2 large handfuls chopped fresh cilantro leaves and fine stems
- Fish sauce, soy sauce, ancho chili powder, black pepper, to taste 

## Method

1. Place the beans in a large bowl and fill with enough cold water to cover by at least 4". Add 1/4c. kosher salt and stir to dissolve. Let soak 8 to 12 hours.  
2. In a large Dutch oven, add the stock, bay leaves, 4 tsp. kosher salt, and epazote (if using). Drain and rinse the beans, adding them to the stock & seasonings. Bring to a boil over high heat, reduce to a bare simmer, cover, and cook until the beans are just tender, about 45 minutes.
3. Begin chopping your bacon into a fine dice while the beans cook, and set aside in a container. Consider wearing gloves while you chop the onions, peppers, and garlic; set the diced onions & peppers in their own bowl, and garlic on the side. Finely chop the cilantro into its own container. 
4. When your prep is done, heat the bacon in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook, stirring constantly, until the fat is rendered and the bacon is just starting to brown around the edges, about 5 minutes. Transfer to a larger pot and add the onion and chiles. Cook, stirring, until softened and just starting to brown, about 4 minutes. Add the garlic and cook, stirring, until fragrant, about 30 seconds. Add the fire-roasted tomatoes and cook, stirring and scraping up browned bits from the bottom of the pan, until the liquid is thick and the mixture begins to sizzle, about 3 minutes.
5. Remove the lid from the Dutch oven, pluck out the bay leaves and epazote stems, then add the bacon-tomato mixture and stir to combine. Place under the broiler for 20-60 minutes, until a scorched top layer forms, adding smokiness. Remove from under the broiler, add the cilantro, and stir to combine.
6. Splash in soy sauce, fish sauce, black pepper, and ancho chili powder and mix well. Sample the flavor and adjust to taste. 
7. Reduce oven heat to 205F, place back into the oven covered, and let simmer until ready to serve.
