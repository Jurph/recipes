# Dan Dan Noodles
_based on_ https://www.budgetbytes.com/dan-dan-noodles/

Makes about 4 servings

## Ingredients

### Pork topping

- 1 Tbsp. sesame oil
- 1 lb. ground pork
- 6 cloves garlic, microplaned
- 3 tsp. fresh ginger, microplaned or grated
- 6 scallions, divided: white ends sliced into thin medallions (reserve green ends for garnish) 
- 1 c. frozen spinach, thawed and squeezed dry
- 1 Tbsp. soy sauce (note: some soy sauce goes in pork topping and sauce) 
- 1 Tbsp. brown sugar (note: some brown sugar goes in pork topping and sauce) 

### Sauce

- 1/2 c. soy sauce 
- 1/4 c. rice vinegar
- 3 Tbsp. creamy peanut butter
- 2 Tbsp. chili crisp or chili oil
- 2 Tbsp. brown sugar
- 1 c. reserved pasta water, as needed

### Noodles and garnish

- 1 lb. thin spaghetti
- 6 scallions, divided: green ends finely chopped
- 1/3 c. dry roasted peanuts, crushed

## Method

This recipe can come together fairly fast if you can execute steps in parallel. The pasta water (1) and pork (2) should be going at same time, while you are getting other ingredients organized. 

1. **Boil the noodles.** Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the spaghetti until just al dente, about 2 minutes less than the package directions. Reserve 1 cup of pasta water, drain the noodles, and set them aside.
2. **Brown the pork.** Heat the oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the pork and press it into the pan. Let it sit undisturbed for a few minutes so the bottom gets browned and a little crisp before breaking it up.
3. **Prepare the sauce base.** In a large mixing bowl, whisk together soy sauce, rice vinegar, peanut butter, chili crisp, and brown sugar until smooth.
4. **Prepare the pork topping flavors.** Stir in the garlic, ginger, soy sauce, white scallions, brown sugar, and spinach in a small bowl. 
5. **Stir-fry the pork.** When the pork has crisped, add in the aromatic spinach and begin stir-frying over medium-high heat. Stir until well combined and lightly browned. Reduce to lowest setting and cover.  
6. **Drain the pasta.** When the noodles are *al dente*, reserve 1c. pasta water and drain the noodles. Splash 1/2c. pasta water into the whisked sauce and continue whisking until smooth. 
7. **Dress the noodles.** Toss the spaghetti in the sauce, splashing in more pasta water as needed. The noodles should be evenly coated, not sticky or gloopy.
8. **Serve.** Divide the noodles into bowls. Top with pork, scallions, and crushed peanuts. Serve warm, or chill and serve cold as a noodle salad.

## Notes

Traditional dan dan noodles use sesame paste and preserved mustard greens. This version swaps in peanut butter and spinach for easier grocery shopping. Fresh spinach works too; use about 2 cups chopped fresh spinach in place of the frozen spinach.
For meal prep, store the pork and noodles separately and add the peanuts and scallions right before eating. The noodles keep 3-4 days in the fridge, and the pork mixture freezes well.
